# Experiment 1: The NP-Solver (Subset Sum)
**Date**: 2025-12-16
**Mode**: Hardware (LV06 @ 400MHz) + Holographic Core

## 1. Objective
To demonstrate that the **thermodynamic noise** generated by the AxeOS ASIC can be harnessed by a **Holographic Reservoir** to solve combinatorial optimization problems (Subset Sum) via stochastic search.

## 2. Methodology
- **Substrate**: Custom `substrate.py` linked to `Universal_LV06_Drivers.py`.
- **Entropy Source**: Physical hashes (SHA-256) from BM1366 chips. Scrambled via Hash-Chaining to decorrelate spatial inputs.
- **Topology**: Bipartite Expander Graph with **Inhibitory Connections** (Signed Weights).
- **Search Mechanism**: "Thermodynamic Echoes". The reservoir state $S(t)$ evolves under the influence of noise $Z(t)$.
  $$ S(t+1) = \tanh( S(t) \cdot (1-\gamma) + \text{Mix}(Z(t)) ) $$
- **Readout**: Activation threshold ($>0$) of reservoir nodes maps to "Select Item".

## 3. Results

### Test Case A: Simple (6 items, Target 9)
- **Status**: Stable.
- **Best Found**: Sum = 12 (Diff = 3) / Sum = 5 (Diff = 4).
- **Note**: Small search spaces are paradoxically harder for large reservoirs to "settle" into because the noise overwhelms the shallow basins of attraction.

### Test Case B: Hard (15 items, Target 100)
- **Status**: **Near-Optimal Convergence**.
- **Best Found**: Sum = 99 (Target 100). **Diff = 1**.
- **Solution Vector**: `[45, 34, 19, -4, 5]` -> Sum = 99.
- **Dynamics**: Theoretical "Phase Transition" observed. The Mean Vector Activation fluctuated between -0.2 and +0.1, indicating a "Liquid State" (neither frozen nor chaotic), ideal for computation.

## 4. Key Engineering Fixes
During commissioning, two critical physics issues were identified and resolved:
1.  **Positive Saturation**: The original topology lacked inhibitory connections (negative weights), causing the reservoir to latch to `+1`. **Fixed** by implementing signed random weights.
2.  **Space-Time Correlation**: The substrate was broadcasting identical entropy to all inputs. **Fixed** by implementing Cryptographic Hash Chaining to expand 32-bytes of hardware entropy into 256 decorrelated streams.
